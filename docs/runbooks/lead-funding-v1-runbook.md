# LEAD Funding V1 Runbook

Use this runbook for the controlled pilot of request-based funding inside LEAD Talent Platform.

## Scope

LEAD Funding v1 helps chapters request support for events or initiatives, receive admin/finance decisions, and close the loop with receipts, evidence, and reflection.

It is not a bank ledger, reimbursement engine, allocation formula, payment processor, or public chapter ranking system.

## Who Can Do What

| Persona | Access |
| --- | --- |
| Chapter president, VP, approved e-board with funding permission | View own chapter requests, create drafts, submit requests, add receipts/evidence, complete post-event reflection. |
| Admin/finance | View all chapter requests, approve, partially approve, reject, request changes, assign internal source, close regularized requests. |
| Regular member | No request-level funding access in v1. |
| Recruiter/company | No funding access. |

## Chapter Flow

1. Open `Capitulo > Financiamiento`.
2. Review active requests and their next action.
3. Click `Nueva solicitud`.
4. Complete required fields:
   - Event or initiative name.
   - Purpose.
   - Event date.
   - Target audience.
   - Requested amount.
   - At least one OKR.
   - At least one impact pillar.
   - Itemized budget that matches the requested amount.
5. Add optional context when helpful:
   - Existing event link.
   - Expected attendance.
   - Partner details.
   - Extra notes.
6. Save as draft or send to review.
7. After approval and event execution, open the request detail.
8. Add actual spend, result/impact summary, receipt notes, and upload receipts or evidence.

## Admin Flow

1. Open `Administracion > Financiamiento`.
2. Use status filters:
   - Pendientes.
   - Cambios solicitados.
   - Aprobadas.
   - Comprobantes.
   - Cerradas.
   - Todas.
3. Review chapter, requester, purpose, OKRs, pillars, date, requested amount, and budget items.
4. Choose one decision:
   - Approve full amount.
   - Approve partial amount.
   - Request changes.
   - Reject.
5. Add an admin decision note when the decision is partial, asks changes, or rejects.
6. Optionally assign internal funding source.
7. When accountability is complete, use `Cerrar solicitud` with a closure note for regularization context.

## Funding Guidance

Usually eligible:

- Moderate food or refreshments.
- Event materials.
- Minimal decorations.
- Learning or program materials.
- Certificates or recognition tied to learning.
- Admin-approved exceptions.

Usually not eligible by default:

- Personal expenses.
- Unapproved merch or giveaways.
- Normal transportation.
- Expenses unrelated to an event or initiative.
- Broad operating costs without a clear LEAD purpose.

## Receipt And Evidence Expectations

The pilot is flexible and non-punitive. Missing receipts do not automatically block future requests in v1, but requests should be regularized so LEAD can understand spend, impact, and learning.

Recommended evidence:

- Receipt or invoice PDF/image.
- Screenshot or link showing attendance, registration, or event output.
- Short impact reflection.
- Actual spend amount.
- Notes explaining exceptions.

Allowed upload formats:

- PDF.
- JPG.
- PNG.
- WebP.
- Maximum 10 MB per file.

## Pilot Operating Notes

- Use a small number of organized chapters first.
- Train chapter leaders live before asking them to submit real requests.
- Keep finance/admin decisions centralized.
- Treat late requests as allowed but flagged.
- Review confusing requests manually instead of adding strict blockers too early.
- Do not publish funding data across chapters during v1.

## Out Of Scope For V1

- Automatic reimbursement.
- Real payment approvals.
- Public financial transparency dashboard.
- Member-submitted requests.
- Chapter funding score.
- LEAD Pulse correlation.
- LEAD Funding integration with external accounting tools.
